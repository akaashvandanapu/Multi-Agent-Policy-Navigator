FERTILIZER MANAGEMENT GUIDE FOR MAJOR CROPS - ANDHRA PRADESH
=============================================================

NOTE: For official soil health and fertilizer recommendations, contact:
- Soil Testing Laboratories (District-wise)
- Department of Agriculture, AP
- National Centre for Organic Farming

BASIC PRINCIPLES OF FERTILIZER MANAGEMENT
=========================================

THREE PRIMARY NUTRIENTS (NPK):
-----------------------------
1. NITROGEN (N) - Leaf Growth
   - Promotes vegetative growth
   - Dark green color
   - Protein synthesis
   
2. PHOSPHORUS (P) - Root Development
   - Root growth and establishment
   - Flowering and fruiting
   - Energy transfer (ATP)
   
3. POTASSIUM (K) - Overall Vigor
   - Disease resistance
   - Water regulation
   - Fruit quality

SECONDARY NUTRIENTS:
-------------------
- Calcium (Ca): Cell wall strength
- Magnesium (Mg): Chlorophyll component
- Sulphur (S): Protein synthesis, oil quality

MICRONUTRIENTS (Trace elements):
--------------------------------
- Zinc (Zn), Iron (Fe), Manganese (Mn)
- Copper (Cu), Boron (B), Molybdenum (Mo)
- Required in small quantities but critical

================================================================================
SECTION 1: MAJOR FERTILIZERS & THEIR NUTRIENT CONTENT
================================================================================

NITROGENOUS FERTILIZERS:
-----------------------
1. UREA (46% N)
   - Most common nitrogen source
   - Quick-acting
   - Hygroscopic (absorbs moisture)
   - Apply in splits, incorporate in soil
   - Cost-effective

2. AMMONIUM SULPHATE (20% N + 24% S)
   - Suitable for alkaline soils
   - Provides both N and S
   - Slightly acidifying

3. CALCIUM AMMONIUM NITRATE (CAN) (25% N)
   - Suitable for acidic soils
   - Provides some calcium
   - More expensive than urea

4. AMMONIUM CHLORIDE (25% N)
   - Not common
   - Can cause chloride toxicity if overused

PHOSPHATIC FERTILIZERS:
----------------------
1. SINGLE SUPER PHOSPHATE (SSP) (16% P₂O₅)
   - Also contains 11% S and 21% Ca
   - Best for oilseeds (groundnut - needs S + Ca)
   - Economical

2. DIAMMONIUM PHOSPHATE (DAP) (18% N + 46% P₂O₅)
   - High analysis fertilizer
   - Provides both N and P
   - Popular for basal application
   - Slightly alkaline

3. MONO AMMONIUM PHOSPHATE (MAP) (11% N + 52% P₂O₅)
   - Higher P than DAP
   - Suitable for high P requirement crops

4. ROCK PHOSPHATE (20-30% P₂O₅)
   - Organic farming
   - Slow-release
   - Suitable for acidic soils

POTASSIC FERTILIZERS:
--------------------
1. MURIATE OF POTASH (MOP/KCl) (60% K₂O)
   - Most common K source
   - Contains chloride
   - Economical

2. SULPHATE OF POTASH (SOP/K₂SO₄) (50% K₂O + 18% S)
   - Chloride-free (better for tobacco, grapes, potato)
   - Provides both K and S
   - More expensive than MOP

COMPLEX/NPK FERTILIZERS:
-----------------------
- NPK 10:26:26 - General purpose
- NPK 12:32:16 - High P requirement
- NPK 17:17:17 - Balanced
- NPK 19:19:19 - Balanced (high analysis)
- NPK 20:20:0:13 - With Sulphur

SECONDARY NUTRIENT FERTILIZERS:
------------------------------
1. GYPSUM (CaSO₄.2H₂O)
   - 12% Ca + 16% S
   - Essential for groundnut, oilseeds
   - Improves soil structure

2. LIME (CaCO₃)
   - Corrects soil acidity
   - Provides calcium
   - Improves soil pH

3. MAGNESIUM SULPHATE (Epsom Salt)
   - 10% Mg + 13% S
   - For Mg-deficient soils

MICRONUTRIENT FERTILIZERS:
-------------------------
1. Zinc Sulphate (ZnSO₄.7H₂O): 21% Zn
2. Ferrous Sulphate (FeSO₄.7H₂O): 19% Fe
3. Manganese Sulphate (MnSO₄.4H₂O): 26% Mn
4. Borax (Na₂B₄O₇.10H₂O): 11% B
5. Copper Sulphate (CuSO₄.5H₂O): 25% Cu

SECTION 2: CROP-WISE FERTILIZER RECOMMENDATIONS
================================================================================

A. CEREALS
----------

1. PADDY (Irrigated)
Dose: 120:60:40 kg NPK/ha

Fertilizer Equivalent:
- Urea: 261 kg/ha
- DAP: 130 kg/ha
- MOP: 67 kg/ha

Application Schedule:
Basal (Day 0):           50% N + 100% P + 100% K
                         = 130 kg Urea + 130 kg DAP + 67 kg MOP

Tillering (21 DAS):      25% N = 65 kg Urea

Panicle Initiation (PI): 25% N = 65 kg Urea
(40-45 DAS)

Micronutrients:
- Zinc Sulphate: 25 kg/ha (basal) if Zn deficient
- Iron: Foliar spray FeSO₄ 0.5% if deficiency

Notes:
- For short duration varieties: Reduce N by 20%
- For hybrid rice: Increase N to 150 kg/ha
- Avoid excess N (causes lodging, pest problems)

2. MAIZE
Dose: 150:75:60 kg NPK/ha

Fertilizer Equivalent:
- Urea: 326 kg/ha
- DAP: 163 kg/ha
- MOP: 100 kg/ha

Application Schedule:
Basal (Day 0):       50% N + 100% P + 100% K
                     = 163 kg Urea + 163 kg DAP + 100 kg MOP

Knee-High (25-30 DAS): 25% N = 82 kg Urea

Tasseling (45-50 DAS): 25% N = 82 kg Urea

Micronutrients:
- Zinc Sulphate: 25 kg/ha (basal) - important for maize
- Boron: 5 kg Borax/ha (for sweet corn)

Foliar Nutrition:
- 2% DAP spray at tasseling and grain filling

Notes:
- Zinc deficiency very common in maize
- Apply Zn if white band on leaves

B. OILSEEDS
-----------

1. GROUNDNUT
Dose: 20-25:60:50 kg NPK/ha

Fertilizer Equivalent:
- Urea: 43-54 kg/ha (or SKIP if good Rhizobium)
- SSP: 375 kg/ha (provides P + S + Ca)
- MOP: 83 kg/ha

Application Schedule:
ALL BASAL (At Sowing):
- Full NPK + FYM 5-7 tonnes/ha
- GYPSUM: 400-500 kg/ha at flowering (35-40 DAS) - MANDATORY

Micronutrients:
- Boron: 5 kg Borax/ha (prevents hollow heart)
- Molybdenum: 1 kg/ha (for nodulation)

Foliar Spray:
- 2% DAP at flowering and pod development

Notes:
- GYPSUM IS CRITICAL for pod filling and oil content
- Low N because it's a legume (fixes nitrogen)
- Use SSP (not DAP) - provides Sulphur
- Never skip gypsum application

2. SUNFLOWER
Dose: 60:90:45 kg NPK/ha

Fertilizer Equivalent:
- Urea: 130 kg/ha
- DAP: 196 kg/ha
- MOP: 75 kg/ha

Application Schedule:
Basal (Day 0):         50% N + 100% P + 100% K
                       = 65 kg Urea + 196 kg DAP + 75 kg MOP

Top Dressing (25 DAS): 50% N = 65 kg Urea

Micronutrients:
- Boron: 5-10 kg Borax/ha (critical for seed filling)

Foliar:
- 0.2% Boric acid spray at flower initiation and grain filling

Notes:
- Boron deficiency causes hollow capitulum
- Don't skip boron application

C. PULSES
---------

1. REDGRAM
Dose: 20-25:50:25 kg NPK/ha

Fertilizer Equivalent:
- Urea: 43-54 kg/ha (or SKIP if Rhizobium)
- SSP: 313 kg/ha
- MOP: 42 kg/ha
- GYPSUM: 125 kg/ha (provides S + Ca)

Application Schedule:
ALL BASAL: Full NPK + FYM 5-7 tonnes/ha
No top dressing needed

Micronutrients:
- Boron: 5 kg Borax/ha (better pod set)

Notes:
- Phosphorus is most critical for nodulation
- Low N (it's a legume)
- Avoid excess N (delays flowering)

2. GREENGRAM/BLACKGRAM
Dose: 25:50:25 kg NPK/ha

Fertilizer Equivalent:
- Urea: 54 kg/ha
- DAP: 109 kg/ha
- MOP: 42 kg/ha

Application Schedule:
ALL BASAL: Full NPK

Micronutrients:
- PSB (Phosphate Solubilizing Bacteria) @ 25 gm/kg seed

Notes:
- All fertilizer at sowing
- Short duration (60-70 days)

D. COMMERCIAL CROPS
-------------------

1. COTTON
Dose: 150:75:75 kg NPK/ha

Fertilizer Equivalent:
- Urea: 326 kg/ha
- DAP: 163 kg/ha
- MOP: 125 kg/ha

Application Schedule:
Basal (Day 0):           50% N + 100% P + 100% K
                         = 163 kg Urea + 163 kg DAP + 125 kg MOP

Square Formation (35 DAS): 25% N = 82 kg Urea

Flowering (55-60 DAS):   25% N = 82 kg Urea

Micronutrients:
- Zinc Sulphate: 25 kg/ha (basal)
- Boron: 5 kg Borax/ha (better boll retention)

Foliar Sprays:
- 1% MgSO₄ + 2% KNO₃ at peak flowering (reduces boll drop)
- 19:19:19 @ 5 gm/liter (2-3 sprays)

Notes:
- Don't apply excess N (causes vegetative growth, pest problems)
- Apply K at flowering for better boll development
- Foliar nutrition improves boll retention

2. CHILLI
Dose: 150:75:75 kg NPK/ha (for 6-month crop)

Fertilizer Equivalent:
- Urea: 326 kg/ha
- DAP: 163 kg/ha
- MOP: 125 kg/ha
- FYM: 25 tonnes/ha (critical)

Application Schedule:
Basal (Before transplanting):
- FYM: 25 tonnes/ha
- 100% P + 50% K = 163 kg DAP + 62 kg MOP

After Transplanting:
- 30 DAT: 33% N + 25% K = 109 kg Urea + 31 kg MOP
- 60 DAT: 33% N + 25% K = 109 kg Urea + 31 kg MOP
- 90 DAT: 33% N = 109 kg Urea

Micronutrients:
- Zinc, Iron, Boron - all critical
- Foliar spray of micronutrient mixture

Notes:
- Chilli is heavy feeder
- Organic matter (FYM) is essential
- Multiple pickings - multiple N applications

SECTION 3: ORGANIC SOURCES OF NUTRIENTS
================================================================================

FARMYARD MANURE (FYM):
---------------------
Nutrient Content: 0.5% N, 0.2% P₂O₅, 0.5% K₂O
Dose: 10-25 tonnes/ha (crop-dependent)
Application: 2-3 weeks before sowing

Benefits:
- Improves soil structure
- Increases water retention
- Adds organic matter
- Slow-release nutrients
- Enhances microbial activity

COMPOST:
-------
Nutrient Content: 0.8% N, 0.5% P₂O₅, 0.8% K₂O
Dose: 5-10 tonnes/ha

Better than FYM (nutrients more available)

VERMICOMPOST:
------------
Nutrient Content: 1.5% N, 1.0% P₂O₅, 1.3% K₂O
Dose: 2.5-5 tonnes/ha

Benefits:
- Higher nutrient content than FYM
- Faster decomposition
- Improves soil biology
- Contains growth hormones

GREEN MANURE:
------------
Crops: Dhaincha, Sunhemp, Cowpea
Method: Grow for 45-60 days, incorporate into soil

Benefits:
- Adds 60-80 kg N/ha (for legumes)
- Improves soil organic matter
- Suppresses weeds
- Breaks disease cycle

BIOFERTILIZERS:
--------------
1. RHIZOBIUM: For legumes (fixes 20-40 kg N/ha)
2. AZOSPIRILLUM: For cereals (fixes 20-25 kg N/ha)
3. AZOTOBACTER: For vegetables (fixes 15-20 kg N/ha)
4. PSB: Solubilizes phosphorus (saves 20-25 kg P/ha)
5. KSB: Mobilizes potassium

Dose: 25 gm/kg seed or 2.5 kg/ha (soil application)

NEEM CAKE:
---------
Nutrient Content: 5% N, 1% P₂O₅, 1.5% K₂O
Dose: 250-500 kg/ha

Benefits:
- Slow-release nitrogen
- Nematicidal properties
- Pest repellent
- Improves soil health

SECTION 4: SOIL TESTING & FERTILIZER ADJUSTMENT
================================================================================

WHY SOIL TESTING?
----------------
- Know exact nutrient status of your soil
- Avoid over-fertilization (saves money)
- Prevent nutrient deficiency (increases yield)
- Balanced fertilization

WHEN TO TEST?
-------------
- Once in 2-3 years
- Best time: Before crop season
- After harvest of previous crop

SOIL SAMPLING:
-------------
1. Collect samples from 10-15 spots (zig-zag pattern)
2. Depth: 0-15 cm (for annual crops)
3. Mix thoroughly, take 500 gm composite sample
4. Send to nearest Soil Testing Laboratory

WHAT IS TESTED?
--------------
- pH (acidity/alkalinity)
- Electrical Conductivity (salinity)
- Organic Carbon
- Available N, P, K
- Micronutrients (if requested)

INTERPRETATION:
--------------
Nutrient Status based on Soil Test:

NITROGEN:
- Low: <280 kg/ha
- Medium: 280-560 kg/ha
- High: >560 kg/ha

PHOSPHORUS (P₂O₅):
- Low: <12 kg/ha
- Medium: 12-25 kg/ha
- High: >25 kg/ha

POTASSIUM (K₂O):
- Low: <110 kg/ha
- Medium: 110-280 kg/ha
- High: >280 kg/ha

FERTILIZER ADJUSTMENT:
---------------------
Based on soil test results, adjust fertilizer dose:

Soil Status    Fertilizer Dose
-----------    ---------------
Low            100% of recommendation + 25%
Medium         100% of recommendation
High           75% of recommendation

Example:
If soil test shows HIGH P, apply only 75% of recommended P fertilizer.
If soil shows LOW K, apply 100% + 25% = 125% of recommended K.

SECTION 5: NUTRIENT DEFICIENCY SYMPTOMS
================================================================================

NITROGEN (N) DEFICIENCY:
-----------------------
Symptoms:
- Yellowing of older leaves (chlorosis)
- Stunted growth
- Thin stems
- Lower yield

Correction: Top dress with Urea @ 50-100 kg/ha OR Foliar spray 2% Urea

PHOSPHORUS (P) DEFICIENCY:
-------------------------
Symptoms:
- Dark green or purplish leaves
- Stunted growth
- Delayed maturity
- Poor root development

Correction: Apply DAP or SSP as per crop recommendation

POTASSIUM (K) DEFICIENCY:
------------------------
Symptoms:
- Yellowing and browning of leaf margins (older leaves first)
- Weak stems (lodging in cereals)
- Poor grain/fruit quality

Correction: Apply MOP @ 50-75 kg/ha OR Foliar spray 1% KNO₃

ZINC (Zn) DEFICIENCY (Very common in AP):
-----------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- White patches on leaves (especially maize)
- Short internodes (rosetting)
- Bronzing in rice

Correction:
- Soil: Zinc Sulphate @ 25 kg/ha
- Foliar: 0.5% ZnSO₄ spray (2-3 times)

IRON (Fe) DEFICIENCY:
--------------------
Symptoms:
- Yellowing of young leaves (interveinal chlorosis)
- Green veins, yellow leaf tissue

Correction: Foliar spray FeSO₄ @ 0.5% (2-3 times)

BORON (B) DEFICIENCY:
--------------------
Symptoms:
- Failure of flowering
- Hollow stem/heart
- Poor fruit/seed set
- In groundnut: Hollow pods

Correction:
- Soil: Borax @ 5-10 kg/ha
- Foliar: Boric acid @ 0.2% at flowering

SULPHUR (S) DEFICIENCY (Common in oilseeds):
--------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Yellowing of young leaves (similar to N but affects young leaves)
- Stunted growth

Correction: Apply Gypsum @ 250-500 kg/ha

SECTION 6: FERTILIZER USE EFFICIENCY (FUE) - BEST PRACTICES
================================================================================

IMPROVE NITROGEN USE EFFICIENCY:
-------------------------------
1. Split Application: Apply N in 2-3 splits (reduces leaching)
2. Deep Placement: Place urea 8-10 cm deep (rice)
3. Coating: Use neem-coated urea (slow release)
4. Right Timing: Apply when crop needs it most
5. Avoid Broadcast on Surface: Volatilization losses

IMPROVE PHOSPHORUS USE EFFICIENCY:
---------------------------------
1. Band Placement: Place near seed/root zone
2. Mix with FYM: Improves P availability
3. Use PSB: Solubilizes fixed P
4. Maintain Soil pH: P availability is best at pH 6.5-7.5

IMPROVE POTASSIUM USE EFFICIENCY:
--------------------------------
1. Apply at Critical Stages: Flowering, fruit development
2. Avoid Surface Application: Incorporate in soil
3. Split Application: For long-duration crops

GENERAL PRACTICES:
-----------------
1. Balanced Fertilization: Apply NPK in right ratio
2. Soil Testing: Apply based on soil test
3. Organic Matter: Use FYM/compost (improves FUE)
4. Biofertilizers: Reduces fertilizer need by 20-25%
5. Proper Irrigation: Nutrients move to roots via water
6. Weed Control: Weeds compete for nutrients

SECTION 7: INTEGRATED NUTRIENT MANAGEMENT (INM)
================================================================================

CONCEPT:
Combination of organic and inorganic sources for balanced nutrition

ADVANTAGES:
- Reduces fertilizer cost by 20-30%
- Improves soil health
- Sustainable agriculture
- Better crop quality

INM APPROACH:

100% NPK Requirement = 50% from Organic + 50% from Chemical

Example for Paddy (120:60:40 NPK/ha):
- Apply FYM @ 10 tonnes/ha (provides 50 kg N + 20 kg P + 50 kg K)
- Remaining from chemical fertilizers:
  * N: 120 - 50 = 70 kg (apply as Urea)
  * P: 60 - 20 = 40 kg (apply as DAP)
  * K: 40 - 50 = 0 (no need for MOP!)

Benefits:
- Saved cost on MOP (not needed)
- Reduced urea use
- Improved soil health

INM COMPONENTS:
1. Organic Manures (FYM, Compost, Vermicompost)
2. Crop Residues (incorporation after harvest)
3. Green Manure (Dhaincha, Sunhemp)
4. Biofertilizers (Rhizobium, Azospirillum, PSB)
5. Chemical Fertilizers (to meet remaining requirement)

SECTION 8: FERTIGATION (Fertilizer through Drip Irrigation)
================================================================================

WHAT IS FERTIGATION?
Application of water-soluble fertilizers through drip irrigation system

ADVANTAGES:
- 30-40% fertilizer saving
- Better nutrient use efficiency
- Uniform application
- Reduced labor
- Timely application

WATER-SOLUBLE FERTILIZERS:
--------------------------
- Urea (N)
- 19:19:19 (NPK)
- 12:61:00 (High P)
- 13:00:45 (High K)
- Potassium Nitrate (13:00:46)
- Mono Ammonium Phosphate (12:61:00)

FERTIGATION SCHEDULE (Example: Drip Chilli):
--------------------------------------------
Total Dose: 150:75:75 kg NPK/ha

Split into weekly applications (20 applications):
- Each week: 7.5 kg N + 3.75 kg P + 3.75 kg K
- Use water-soluble NPK through drip
- Last 30 minutes of irrigation

Benefits:
- Precise nutrient delivery
- Reduced nutrient losses
- Higher yield (20-30% increase)

SUITABLE CROPS:
- All drip-irrigated crops (vegetables, cotton, sugarcane)

SECTION 9: GOVERNMENT SUPPORT & SUBSIDY
================================================================================

SUBSIDY ON FERTILIZERS:
----------------------
- Urea: Highly subsidized (MRP fixed by Govt.)
- DAP, MOP, NPK: Partial subsidy

DBT SCHEME (Direct Benefit Transfer):
- Subsidy directly to manufacturers
- Farmers buy at subsidized MRP
- Aadhaar seeding mandatory in some states

SUBSIDY ON ORGANIC INPUTS:
-------------------------
Under Various Schemes:
- 50% subsidy on vermicompost units
- Subsidy on bio-fertilizer production
- Support for composting infrastructure

NEEM-COATED UREA:
----------------
- Mandatory for all domestic urea production
- Reduces N losses by 20-30%
- Same price as normal urea

SOIL HEALTH CARD:
----------------
- Free soil testing (once in 2-3 years)
- Crop-wise fertilizer recommendation
- Available at Rythu Seva Kendralu (RSK)

SECTION 10: STORAGE & HANDLING
================================================================================

FERTILIZER STORAGE:
------------------
1. Store in dry, well-ventilated place
2. Keep away from direct sunlight
3. Stack bags on wooden pallets (not on bare floor)
4. Maximum stack height: 10-12 bags
5. Keep away from seeds, food grains
6. Check for moisture/caking periodically

SHELF LIFE:
----------
- Urea: 2-3 years (if stored properly)
- DAP/MOP: 2-3 years
- Complex fertilizers: 1-2 years
- Bio-fertilizers: 6 months (refrigerated)

HANDLING PRECAUTIONS:
--------------------
- Avoid skin contact with fertilizers
- Wash hands after handling
- Don't inhale fertilizer dust
- Use mask if handling large quantities
